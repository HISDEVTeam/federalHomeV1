Sangam literature: The Tamizhakam tales that offer a window into ancient Tamil life

Thirayattam was an ethnic folk art performed only by men, considered a taboo for women. But Seetha Sathish, who grew up watching Thirayattam at her father’s ancestral home in Kozhikode, developed a sense of attachment with the art form. When Seetha started imitating the steps of Thirayattam, her family told her to not invite the wrath of God. It was during her college days that Seetha met Moorkkanad Peethambaran, a traditional Thirayattam performer, and asked him to teach her. Both received threats for ‘breaking tradition’. Six months ago, Seetha first performed Thirayattam at a public park in Kozhikode. Click her to know how Seetha is preparing to take the art form to the global stage. Satish Narayan & Nandini Bose
